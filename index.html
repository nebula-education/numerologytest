What is
astrology?

Section 01 · Foundation

A symbolic language
between sky and life

At its core, astrology is a system that studies the link between movements in the sky — the Sun, the Moon, and the planets — and patterns in human life. Its roots go back to ancient Babylon, Greece, and Egypt.

Astrology works as a symbolic language — a way of describing life through symbols and patterns, not through fixed predictions. It helps us notice patterns, personality traits, and timing.

You can think of it as a tool for awareness — a way to recognize your habits, cycles, and what drives you on a deeper level.

Three layers, one system

How a chart is read

Every chart reading combines three layers. Each one answers a different question, and only together do they tell the full story.

1
Planets — what is happening The active forces. Each planet stands for a specific drive, function, or part of your personality.
2
Signs — how that energy is expressed The personality flavour. A sign colours and shapes the way a planet acts.
3
Houses — where it shows up in life The life area. Each house points to a specific domain — home, career, relationships, and so on.

Section 02 · Signs

Twelve types of energy

The zodiac is a 360-degree circle in the sky, divided into 12 equal parts. Each part is one zodiac sign — a "type of energy" with its own personality and its own way of expressing itself. Together, from Aries to Pisces, they form a complete system.

You can think of the zodiac as a full range of human experience — different ways we act, feel, and respond to the world. No sign is better or worse than another. Each one simply expresses energy in its own way.

How signs work

Elements and modalities

To understand the signs better, we group them by elements and modalities. An element describes the basic nature of a sign. A modality describes how a sign acts. Combine an element and a modality, and you get a much clearer picture of how a sign actually behaves.

💨

💧

Three modalities
⇢

Cardinal

Initiators. Cardinal signs start things and set them in motion.

◆

Fixed

Builders. Fixed signs keep things steady and build on what's there.

∽

Adapters. Mutable signs shift, adjust, and go with the flow.

The four families

Fire, Earth, Air, Water

Each element holds three signs. Read together, they show how the zodiac balances vision and action, thinking and feeling.

Element

Fire signs

Aries · Leo · Sagittarius — energetic, bold, and driven by inspiration. Fire brings the ideas and the spark.

Element

Earth signs

Taurus · Virgo · Capricorn — grounded, practical, focused on stability and real results. Earth turns ideas into something real.

Element

Air signs

Gemini · Libra · Aquarius — intellectual and social. Air processes life through ideas and conversation.

Element

Water signs

Cancer · Scorpio · Pisces — emotional and intuitive. Water experiences life through feelings and inner sensing.

In short

Fire and earth balance vision and action. Air and water balance thinking and feeling. One pair gives momentum, the other gives meaning — and that's the rhythm of any chart.

The

Section 03 · Active forces

What the planets do

If signs describe the personality of energy, planets are the active forces themselves. Each planet stands for a specific drive — a part of you, or a process at work in your life.

A quick note: the Sun and Moon aren't technically planets, but in astrology we group them with planets, because they work the same way in a chart.

Planets express themselves through zodiac signs, and they act within specific areas of life called houses. So: signs show how, planets show what, and houses show where. This is one of the most important ideas to remember.

Inner planets

Your personal, day-to-day self

The inner planets are the Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, and Mars. They move quickly and shape your personal personality the most — which is why they matter so much in personal readings.

☉ Sun Core identity tap to reveal
Who you are at heart

☾ Moon Inner world tap to reveal
Emotions and safety

The Moon reflects your emotional world — what you need to feel safe, soothed, and at home in yourself.

☿ Mercury Mind tap to reveal
How you think and speak

Mercury shows how you take in information, how you reason, and how you share ideas with others.

♀ Venus Love & values tap to reveal
What you find beautiful

Venus is about love, attraction, values, and pleasure. She shows what you're drawn to and what you enjoy.

♂ Mars Drive tap to reveal
Action and conflict

Mars stands for drive, ambition, and how you handle conflict. The "go" energy that turns desire into motion.

Outer planets

The slow, generational forces

The outer planets are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto. They move much more slowly and describe bigger themes — the ones that shape us over years and often shape entire generations.

♃ Jupiter Growth tap to reveal
Expansion and meaning

Jupiter is about growth, luck, and what you believe in. Where Jupiter lands, life feels generous and broadens.

♄ Saturn Structure tap to reveal
Discipline and time

Saturn stands for structure, discipline, and long-term lessons. The teacher of the chart — slow, demanding, but lasting.

♅ Uranus Change tap to reveal
Surprise and freedom

Uranus brings change, surprises, and new ideas. The disruptor — where the old patterns break to make room for the next thing.

♆ Neptune Dreams tap to reveal
Imagination and illusion

Neptune is linked to dreams, imagination, and illusions. The most subtle planet — softening the edges, sometimes blurring them.

♇ Pluto Power tap to reveal
Deep transformation

Pluto stands for deep transformation and hidden power. The slow burn that reshapes us from the inside out.

Section 05 · Houses

Where the energy shows up

A house is one of twelve sections of a birth chart. Each house is tied to a specific area of life — like home, career, or relationships. Houses give context to everything else in the chart.

Houses are calculated from your exact birth time, which is why accurate birth data really matters. A few minutes' difference can shift the entire house structure — and with it, where each planet's energy lands in your life.

Key houses to know first

The four anchors

Some houses are especially useful to know early on. These four often point to the main themes in a person's life.

House 1

House 7

Bringing
It Together

Section 06 · Reading the chart

The full astrological story

In astrology, we always read a chart as a combination of three layers. When you combine them, you start to see the full story. This is the moment when astrology turns from theory into real chart reading.

Planets

What

The active force. What is happening — which drive, which function is at play.

Signs

How

The flavour. How that energy is being expressed — its tone, style, and personality.

Houses

Where

The life area. Where in life this is showing up — career, home, relationships, and so on.

"Your birth chart is like a cosmic map. It reflects your patterns, your potential, and the way different parts of your personality interact."

— Foundations of Astrology

Where to go from here. Signs, planets, and houses don't work separately — they work together as one system. As you keep learning, focus on practicing how to combine all three layers. That's where real understanding — and confident chart reading — begins.
